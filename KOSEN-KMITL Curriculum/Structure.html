5-Year Intense Curriculum

Our program is designed to transform 15-year-old students into professional engineers by the age of 20.

Year 1: Foundations

The first year focuses on building a strong foundation in Mathematics, Physics, and English. Students are introduced to basic engineering concepts and the "Monozukuri" (art of making things) philosophy. Laboratories include basic circuit analysis and workshop practice.

Key Subjects: Calculus I, Physics for Engineers, Intro to Programming.

Year 2: Specialized Core

Students begin to dive deeper into their chosen majors (Mechatronics, Computer, Electrical). More complex projects are introduced. The curriculum aligns strictly with the NIT (National Institute of Technology) Japan standard.

Key Subjects: Digital Circuits, Mechanics, Advanced Programming.

Year 3: Advanced Application

The coursework becomes heavily project-based. Students learn to apply theoretical knowledge to solve real-world problems. Interdisciplinary projects are encouraged to foster teamwork and innovation.

Key Subjects: Microcontrollers, Control Systems, Signal Processing.

Year 4: Research & Development

Students start working on pre-graduation projects and engage in research. Advanced topics such as AI, IoT, and Robotics are covered in depth.

Key Subjects: Robotics, Artificial Intelligence, Industrial Automation.

Year 5: Graduation & Internship

The final year emphasizes the Capstone Project and Industrial Internship. Students spend significant time in the industry to gain practical experience. Many students also have the opportunity to participate in exchange programs with KOSEN colleges in Japan.

Key Subjects: Capstone Project, Seminar, Industrial Training.
